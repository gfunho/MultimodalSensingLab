The Multimodal Sensing Lab (MSL), led by Prof. Miguel Bordallo López, is an interdisciplinary research group at the University of Oulu.

We focus on creating intelligent systems that fuse data from disparate sensing modalities—primarily vision, radio (RF), and physiological signals—to derive robust and actionable insights. Our work spans the entire pipeline, from novel data acquisition and hardware implementation to advanced signal processing and deep learning.

Key Research Themes

Our work is organized into four primary pillars of innovation.

Remote Health & Biosignals

Non-invasive, contactless monitoring of physiological signals (rPPG, respiration) for healthcare diagnostics and well-being.

6G & Digital Twinning

Convergence of communication and sensing (JCAS) to create high-fidelity, radio-compatible Digital Twins using ray tracing.

Robotics & Vision

Robust 3D reconstruction, kinematic analysis, and signal processing for autonomous systems and industrial automation.

Identity & Biometrics

Advanced analysis of human faces for kinship verification, affective computing, and psychological assessment.

Top Innovations &
Impact

Our lab has consistently pushed the boundaries of what is possible with non-contact sensing, from diagnosing depression via video to turning Wi-Fi signals into 3D cameras.

"We don't just analyze data; we create new ways for machines to perceive the world."

- Miguel Bordallo López

01

Radio Propagation as a Service (2025)

Pioneered Differentiable Ray Tracing with point clouds, allowing camera data to directly and accurately simulate radio channels for 6G.

02

Vision from Wi-Fi (2025)

Created CSI2Depth, a method to generate 3D point clouds and depth maps solely from Wi-Fi signals using Transformers.

03

Contactless Depression Detection (2023)

First large-scale demonstration of using rPPG (video-based heart activity) as the primary signal for diagnosing depression.

04

Unsupervised Bio-Signal Extraction (2023)

Developed Face2PPG, an unsupervised pipeline removing the need for medical ground truth data in training remote health models.
